\section{Literature Review}

\subsection{Social Dynamics and the Formation of Authentic Groups}
The core objective of project \textbf{TRIBAL} is to facilitate the creation of \textbf{authentic social groups} in urban environments. In the field of developmental and social psychology, these groups are defined as voluntary social relations that provide essential "cognitive and social scaffolding". Research indicates that individuals, especially in transitional life stages, prioritize these peer relationships over formal or professional interactions because they offer a unique sense of intimacy, trust, and validation. In a new city, the absence of a pre-existing social circle can lead to isolation and psychological distress. By integrating roommate matching with local activity groups, \textbf{TRIBAL} aims to replicate the "mutual support" systems identified in literature, where individuals voluntarily come together to address common needs and share social concerns.

\subsection{Limitations of Traditional Matching Frameworks}
\subsubsection{The Vocabulary Mismatch and Cold-Start Problem}
Existing digital platforms for roommate and social discovery frequently struggle with two primary technical hurdles: \textbf{vocabulary mismatch} and the \textbf{cold-start problem}. Vocabulary mismatch occurs when a system fails to link relevant users because their self-descriptions or lifestyle tags use synonymous but non-identical terms (e.g., "early riser" vs. "morning person"). Furthermore, many collaborative filtering systems suffer from a cold-start, meaning they cannot provide accurate recommendations for new users who have not yet interacted with the platform. \textbf{TRIBAL} addresses these issues by implementing a \textbf{compatibility scoring algorithm} based on structured lifestyle questions—such as sleep schedules, cleanliness, and pet preferences—which serves as a primary data ingestion layer to bypass the lack of historical interaction data.

\subsubsection{Syntactic vs. Semantic Matching Paradigms}
Traditional systems often rely on exact lexical matching, which lacks the depth required to capture the "semantic essence" of human compatibility. Recent advancements in \textbf{Transformer architectures} have revolutionized this field by replacing sequential processing with \textbf{self-attention mechanisms}. This allows systems to represent user profiles as \textbf{dense numerical vectors} in a high-dimensional space. By calculating \textbf{cosine similarity} between these vectors, a platform can identify conceptual alignment between potential roommates even when their terminology differs. \textbf{TRIBAL's} backend logic aligns with this modern shift toward meaning-based rather than symbol-based matching.

\subsection{Digital Platforms and Real-Time Interaction}
\subsubsection{The Efficacy of Mobile-Based Interventions}
The transition to mobile and remote platforms has proven highly effective for fostering social connectedness. Studies on \textbf{mHealth} and remote social tools demonstrate that digital delivery—such as \textbf{Flutter-based} mobile applications—can provide scalable support by equipping users with information and tools to manage their social environments. The use of real-time communication is a critical "active ingredient" in maintaining these digital ties. \textbf{TRIBAL} leverages this by implementing \textbf{WebSockets} and \textbf{Django Channels}, allowing for the instantaneous exchange of information that mimics the fluid nature of face-to-face social interactions.

\subsubsection{Building Trust through Modular Architectures}
Creating a "trusted community" requires a secure and modular technical framework. Literature on modular LLM and application orchestration suggests that utilizing a robust backend—similar to \textbf{TRIBAL's} use of \textbf{Django} and \textbf{PostgreSQL}—ensures that recommendations are factually grounded and data-secure. The integration of \textbf{JWT tokens} for authentication provides the necessary security layer to foster a "trusted" environment. By combining a \textbf{vectorized compatibility engine} with real-time social networking, \textbf{TRIBAL} addresses a substantial gap where previous platforms focused only on isolated facets of the urban relocation experience.

\subsection{Conclusion}
Project \textbf{TRIBAL} bridges the gap between purely functional roommate searches and purely social networking platforms. By leveraging the psychological importance of \textbf{authentic social groups} and modern \textbf{similarity retrieval algorithms}, the application provides a comprehensive solution for individuals seeking to rebuild their social scaffolding in a new city.
